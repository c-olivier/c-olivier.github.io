PERSPECTIVES

1 Freedom â€” One can consider freedom as a specialised application of the notion of entropy. This leads to the following absolute measure:
Freedom is the number of choices available or perceived to be available to an individual at any given time.
From this definition an order can be derived:
An individual A is freer than an individual B if A possesses all choices of B and some more.
Relating to the way freedoms are traded:
An exchange of freedoms can be said to be favorable to an individual or a group if the perception of the total value of conceded choices is inferior to that of gained choices.
Note that the measure of freedom is absolute whereas the measure of the exchange is relative to each individual and fluid. I propose that any voluntary association from one individual or group to another individual or group is in general favorable to both parties in the sense described above. One could think of analysing the associations between man and woman, man and society, etc. under this lens of compromised freedoms.
